Shasshrets Tssharyssen Kregtshyssha

A scholar and practitioner of the Way with unusual rituals and knowledge of attunement.

Background

Shasshrets Tssharyssen Kregtshyssha (pronunciation: Shaass-shrets tss-haa-ryss-sen kreg-tshiss-shaa) — called “Shess”.

Overview

Names among the Kharuun‑rooted people are functional: the three parts of the full name describe a role rather than mere ornament. Shess’s full name means: “the wind who watches; the foliage that shields the family; the trunk that will not break.” Other species simplify it to “The family’s Chief and Shield.”

Oath & Taboo

The Roots Oath (Kharuun: Tssh‑Khaarun Ssesshra): “Whoever seeks shelter beneath the foliage, I will not turn my back to the cut. While a single root lives, I will stand before it.” This oath obliges Shess never to abandon those under his protection and forbids participation in operations that deliberately destroy living green in the presence of the defenseless.

Practical Meaning

  • “Family” includes crew, wards, refugees, and anyone under his protection.
  • If forced to choose, family takes priority over mission objectives.
  • Violation of the oath results in cultural and psychological collapse: loss of purpose and standing (the Kharuun say the wind no longer points the way).

Character Snapshot

Shess is deeply loyal but slow to bind. Loyalty for him is assumed responsibility, not an emotional flourish. He avoids conflict unless core values are at stake (family, the defenseless, or the living environment); when he acts, he closes the problem decisively and permanently.

Methods & Conduct

  • Prefers non‑escalatory, final actions: sabotage, disabling equipment, marking boundaries.
  • Uses etiquette and courtesy as tactical tools: planting before speaking, removing boots as sign of guesthood.
  • In combat, he positions himself before the most vulnerable and accepts risk to protect others.

Hegemony Status

Recorded derisively as SHESS / “SHRUBCHIEF” (Hegemony Registry). Wanted for sabotage and obstruction of green‑zone protocols; assessed medium priority due to cost of capture. File notes: “Excessive family loyalty. Not intimidated. Increased aggression when environmental destruction is present.”

Notable Incident — “The Tssharyssen Event”

At Kelsharr Prime (industrial biosphere), Shess sabotaged biochem burning units and stopped logging platforms by hand, evacuating local communities without casualties. The operation’s failure cost the Hegemony prestige and material loss and placed Shess on the wanted list.

Vignettes

  • Negotiation: he replants the room’s ornamental plant into soil and declares “Now there is life here. We can talk.”
  • Dock standoff: he kneels, palms to the floor, and uses a torn pillar as a boundary — the opposing militia withdraws.
  • Bar: he quietly snuffs a cigarette ember in a plant and returns to his drink — no escalation.

Quick Reference

  • Core rule: If he accepts you, you are family — permanently.
  • Behavior: Loyal, rarely social, decisive when values are violated.
  • Combat: Marks boundaries, disables threats, closes conflicts rather than pursues.

One‑line

Shasshrets Tssharyssen Kregtshyssha is not a savior or peacemaker; he is the protective Chief who preserves what remains alive and will not let it be destroyed.

Traits

  • Deeply attuned to the Way
  • Scholarly and secretive
  • Prone to cryptic pronouncements

Gear & Notes

Personal talismans, memory-bound ritual notes, and a network of mystics. Proceed with caution around Ur relics — Shasshrets treats them as living things.
