Wes Galen

Independent trader and salvage operator known for locating unusual relic components and navigating hazardous salvage fields.

Background

Heritage & Background

Wes Galen’s parents are high‑ranking Hegemony officials. His father, Toryn, is a military general, and his mother, Grea, holds a senior position in the imperial tax authority. Wes spent his childhood in the Core worlds in relative luxury thanks to his parents’ status and was a regular at lavish balls and galas hosted by the highest circles of society. As a teenager he joined the navy; after completing officer training he expected a secure career. Two years after leaving the academy he was named XO (executive officer) aboard the naval vessel HG‑Zodia, bound for the Procyon system under Captain Dolomius Reyn.

Charming and a smooth talker, Wes was well liked by much of the Zodia’s crew and often mitigated the effects of his captain’s harsh measures through empathy. At first he felt at home in the middleman role in social situations. Over time he became disillusioned as he witnessed the callous oppression and brutal policies enacted by senior command: blockades labeled as “tax negotiations,” executions of mining supervisors for demanding better pay and conditions, and other abuses.

Tormented by guilt, he began organising covert relief efforts: supplying towns, mining colonies, medical stations, refugee camps and other settlements that had been deprived by imperial actions. He carefully cultivated contacts with people who had the skills and connections for this work—miners, hackers, smugglers, information brokers, local thugs, diplomats and minor politicians—then doctored logs, manifests and charts to redistribute medicine, food, water and equipment to communities he believed had been robbed by the Hegemony.

He was eventually exposed—betrayed by a doctor he had trusted to help smuggle medical equipment to an orbital mining facility. Because of his father’s rank he was spared a long public trial and was instead sent directly to the high‑security Isotropa prison complex. He is currently incarcerated there, planning an escape and forging new alliances with the aim of continuing his work against Hegemony oppression.

Beliefs

  • Everyone has the right to determine their own fate.
  • People have the right to freely choose their leaders and to leave a community if they disagree with its majority.
  • “Nothing about us without us.”
  • Expertise cannot rest solely on theory—practical experience is required to truly understand a field.
  • Freedom of expression is a basic right for any sentient being, however radical or repugnant their views may be.
  • People are more important than the systems they serve.

Goals

  • Expand self‑determination rights and establish democratic systems where autocracy now rules.
  • Break the Hegemony’s rule, at least within the Procyon sector.
  • Free Yaru clones and any sentient beings treated as slaves.
  • Reduce the number of weapons capable of mass destruction—if faceless mass killing is possible at a distance, people are less likely to negotiate.

Core Personality Traits

  • He genuinely likes people; when an individual wins his heart he trusts them almost blindly.
  • Open and honest in expressing emotions.
  • Idealism verging on naivety.
  • Curious about people and their stories; sees the individual behind rank and labels.
  • Believes most people can be reasoned with; values redemption and persuasion.
  • Calm in crisis—the greater the chaos, the calmer he becomes.
  • Seeks solutions, not scapegoats, when things go wrong.
  • Publicly takes responsibility for his crew; holds accountability privately.
  • Protects his people under all circumstances and asks nothing of others he wouldn’t do himself.
  • Organizes people quickly and effectively; only commands when required and involves others when possible.
  • Listens to experts; has strong memory for names, faces and social details.
  • Prefers honest persuasion before deception.
  • Rarely enraged, but abuse of the vulnerable or misuse of power provokes a cold, focused anger that can lead to rash decisions.
  • Leadership credo: “I don’t need to be the smartest in the room. My job is to get the most out of the smartest.”

Habits & Mannerisms

  • Leaves spectacular messes—his cabin is chaotic and he constantly misplaces things that later turn up in impossible places.
  • Sits informally unless decorum forces him otherwise—crosses his leg on armrests, perches on crates, railings, engine covers.
  • Despite a pleasant singing voice and perfect pitch, he loudly listens to abrasive noise music and often hums tunes under his breath.
  • Collects objects, trophies and stories wherever he goes.
  • Frequently offers food to guests, conjuring snacks from the depths of his chaos; enthusiastic if not a skilled cook.
  • Quickly adopts local slang and feels at home in alien environments.
  • Treats everyone as an equal in conversation; gestures reflect relation rather than social rank.

Flaws & Blind Spots

  • Tends to trust others too easily and give second chances, which can be exploited.
  • Believes understanding motives equals ability to persuade—often not true.
  • Has difficulty abandoning former allies and underestimates others’ capacity for cruelty.
  • Assumes people are basically rational and sometimes forgets not everyone had his opportunities or upbringing.
