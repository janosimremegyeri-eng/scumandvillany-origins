Technology in Procyon Sector

The people of Procyon Sector live in a civilization that is advanced, practical, and deeply constrained. Their science is not magical, but it is shaped by relics, scarcity, and political control.

Core Principles
  • No faster-than-light travel and limited communication
  • Relic-bound progress and incomplete replication
  • Physical data remains common and trusted
  • AI is rare and tied to Urbot artifacts

The Price of Progress

The technologies of Procyon Sector are not primitive, but neither are they free to develop. Many of the foundational systems that would normally drive rapid industrial growth were discovered in the form of ancient Ur relics. These relics are useful, powerful, and often essential, but they remain only partially understood. Their principles can be exploited, but not fully reproduced. Because of that, the civilization advances through adaptation, patchwork engineering, and careful maintenance rather than clean, scalable replacement.

Ships and Space Travel

Ships in the sector are built for practical travel across a dangerous frontier, but the most important routes are governed by the Hegemony. The region is connected by gateways, ancient and maintained transit points that allow ships to cross vast distances far faster than conventional travel. These gateways are tightly controlled, heavily monitored, and rarely available to those outside imperial or state authority. Ordinary ships can still move between worlds through conventional routes, but such journeys are slow, costly, and vulnerable to delay. The true strategic advantage lies in access to the gateways and the ability to move through them safely.

Communication and the Network

The public information system is known simply as The Network. Each planet maintains its own infrastructure of connected cities, exchange stations, and local services. It is useful for commerce, administration, and everyday life, but it is also watched, filtered, and controlled. The Hegemony can regulate access, monitor traffic, and restrict sensitive data. Beyond the visible Network lie hidden regions, untrusted channels, and closed systems that are only accessible through direct methods or special credentials.

Artificial Intelligence

There is no software-only intelligence in the conventional sense. Any AI, whether housed in an android body, a station, a ship, or a manor estate, requires an Urbot artifact as its core. Without that artifact, the machine may be capable of processing, but not of true cognition. This makes AI rare, expensive, and politically significant. It is never considered ordinary.

Data in a Controlled Age

Because digital systems are monitored and vulnerable, many people still rely on physical storage. Memory crystals, sealed capsules, and hand-carried archives are common ways to move sensitive information. They are portable, discreet, and difficult to intercept. In a culture where networks can be watched and corrupted, physical data remains trusted and valuable.

Key Technologies and Their Limits

  • Arm computers: essential tools for personal access, communication, and navigation, but dependent on stable power and network availability.
  • Conventional ship drives: practical and common, but limited by fuel, mass, and travel time.
  • Jump drives: require a contained Ur artifact to function and are restricted to those who can secure or maintain them.
  • Urban Networks: efficient for daily life, but controlled by authorities and vulnerable to surveillance.
  • Urbot cores: the basis of advanced intelligence, but rare, difficult to maintain, and never mass-produced.
  • Memory crystals: trusted storage for secrets and records, but slow to access and easy to lose.
