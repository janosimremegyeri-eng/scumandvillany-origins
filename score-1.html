Starting Position (excerpt you provided)

THE SITUATION Your crew was framed and caught for crimes that—for once—you have not committed. They’ve sent you all to the nastiest prison in the sector—Isotropa Max Secure. Whether you knew each other already, or made friends in the scant few days here, you’ve forged an alliance, and a plan. One of you has a ship held in impound—called the Firedrake—until the Starsmiths can pick it up. The Hegemony techs have secured it, but you have hidden security overrides and ignition codes if you can get onboard. It’s just the chance you need to get out before the Hegemony factions figure out which crimes you actually committed. Tonight is the night you spring your plan into action. It’s simple: get free, get your stuff, get the ship, get out of dodge. Mayhem, profit, and rebellion are optional. Can you recruit any other prisoners? Will you get out quietly or are you going to make the Hegemonic News Network? And how will you get the ship out of impound? We play to find out.

Primary Objectives

  • Escape Isotropa Max Secure.
  • Recover the crew's gear from the impound safe.
  • Get aboard the Firedrake and depart the system.

Secondary Objectives (optional)

  • Free a valuable prisoner who can repay the crew later.
  • Sabotage prison comms to delay pursuit.
  • Grab one high-value item (data chip, prototype, or credits).

Complications

  • Security drones patrol the corridors—armoured and lethal.
  • An unexpected lockdown door blocks access to the impound.
  • A Syndicate cellmate is offered a bribe to betray escapees.
  • Hegemony Starsmiths arrive early to collect the Firedrake.

Locations

  • Mess Hall — first scene; crowded, loud, then dark.
  • Cell Block — where allies/enemies are held.
  • Corridors & Vents — stealth options and chokepoints.
  • Control Room — terminal to hack drones and doors.
  • Impound Hangar — where the Firedrake waits under guard.

Factions

  • Syndicate Cell — Organized criminals, fences and muscle; trade contraband, run protection. (~50 prisoners)
  • Techs & Starsmith Apprentices — Skilled hands and hackers; can jury‑rig locks and spoof manifests. (~40 prisoners)
  • Military Mutineers & Refuseniks — Ex‑military who resisted illegal orders; disciplined and dangerous. (~35 prisoners)
  • Political Dissidents — Activists and relief runners; networks and persuasive organizers. (~45 prisoners)
  • Cult / Way Circles — Mystics and sect survivors; secret knowledge and unpredictable loyalties. (~25 prisoners)
  • Scavengers & Smugglers — Small crews, quick hands, lockpicks and small craft contacts. (~35 prisoners)
  • Lifer / Barter Class — Long‑term inmates who run contraband markets and broker favors. (~20–30 prisoners)

These factions control most of the block’s economy, favors, and information. The crew will need to navigate loyalties, payoffs, and rivalries to recruit help for the breakout.

Key NPCs

  • Warden Kress — gruff, slow to trust; radios reinforcements.
  • Tech Sergeant Ril — in charge of drones and impound locks.
  • Starsmith Agent — intends to tow the Firedrake; will escalate if engaged.
  • Blish “Cut” Marv — Syndicate power player in the block; influential, will trade muscle or favors for payment.
  • Hana Vell — young Starsmith apprentice (Techs faction); can spoof impound manifests and hack terminals.
  • Luka Vens — ex‑squad leader (Mutineers); knows patrol patterns and vent access, good for tactical diversions.
  • Greta Q. — political dissident organizer; can rally sympathetic prisoners and negotiate favors.
  • Ishra T’Kell — Way adherent and sect survivor; provides ritual distractions or cryptic leverage.
  • Jax Corin — fence and scavenger; supplies makeshift EMPs, forged keys, and contacts outside the prison.
  • Sulep “Soot” Marren — lifer and info broker; controls contraband networks and knows who owes what.

GM Notes & Mechanics

  • Suggested Clocks: Alarm (6 segments), Drone Patrol (4), Starsmith Arrival (3).
  • Allow makeshift gear—bribes, smuggled EMPs, forged keys—chosen when they reach their stuff.
  • Stealth, hacking, and direct action are all viable; offer skill challenges and consequences linked to the clocks.
  • If players free high-profile prisoners, introduce a followup faction-ally or bounty-clock.

First Scene Script

Read to players (setup):

“You’re in the mess on a normal day, eating food, stretching your legs, getting into fights while the guards watch… But you know that today’s the day. There’s a blare of alarms followed by silence. Darkness falls, and it will be a few minutes before anything but emergency lights turn on. In the commotion, a few cell doors spring open. Your gear is in a safe, past a grate and down the hall. The guards are confused, trying to find their way without lights. The drones that back them up are the real problem. How do you get past them both and deal with the door leading to your stuff?”

Session End & Followups

Session ends when the crew is free of the prison. Use downtime to resolve ship repairs, heat, and bounty pressure. Possible followups: discover who framed them, Syndicate favors owed, Starsmith pursuit.

Use this as a template—personalize the Firedrake owner, allied prisoner, and immediate rival before play.
