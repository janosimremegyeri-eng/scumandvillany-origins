Scum and Villainy — Lore Summary

Overview
- Setting: Procyon Sector within the Galactic Hegemonic Alliance (the Hegemony).
- Tone: Space-western frontier — pragmatic, slightly grim, archival, and political.

The Hegemony
- Central authority: the Hegemon + Grand Council (seven Noble Houses, High Guilds, Hegemonic Cults).
- Enforcement: a massive space navy with legions in each sector.
- Powerful Guilds: Guild of Engineers (mining, AI/cybernetics), Starsmiths Guild (jump drives, shipbuilding), Counters Guild (banking/credits), Makers Guild/Yaru (cloning/genetics).

The Way
- A metaphysical phenomenon interacting with energy and gravity; some beings and mystics can Attune to it.
- Cults and sanctioned mystics exist; attunement shapes culture and gameplay.

Technology & Ur relics
- Precursor (Ur) artifacts underpin advanced technology (jumpgates, Urbot cores, relics).
- Jumpgates and jump drives enable FTL transit; regulation and guild control of drives and gates is politically significant.
- Urbot cores can grant limited sentience to machines; AIs are rare and artifact-dependent.
- Physical storage (memory crystals, sealed archives) remains valuable due to network surveillance.

Communications
- Ansibles (patented by the Sah'iir) provide fast in-system comms; inter-system comms need open gates or couriers.
- The public Network exists but is monitored and filtered by authorities.

Procyon Sector (high level)
- Four systems connected by jumpgates: Rin (gateway; Aleph, Warren; SB-176), Holt (Sonhandra, Mem), Iota (industrial; Amerath, Indri, Shipyards, Lithios), Brekk (culture; Shimaya, Nightfall, Aketi preserve, Isotropa Max prison).
- Governor: Ritam al'Malklaith; 51st Legion enforces order locally.
- Economy: resource extraction, shipyards, manufacturing; strong Guild presence.

Crime, Factions & Notables
- Syndicates: Scarlet Wolves, Ashen Knives; pirates: Maelstrom, Draxler's Raiders.
- Notable figures/roles: information brokers (e.g., Vorex), Guild operatives, Hegemonic officers.

Ships & Economy
- Ship classes: personal, freighter, corvette, frigate, dreadnought; capital ships are rare in the sector.
- Currency: CRED managed by the Counters Guild; local prices are intentionally low relative to Guild wealth.

GM Notes / Hooks
- Gate access, Ur relic acquisition, and control of Urbot cores are high-stakes political objectives.
- Smuggling, data courier runs (physical memory crystals), and covert attunement rituals make strong mission seeds.
- Tech scarcity and Guild patents create friction and moral choices for player crews.
