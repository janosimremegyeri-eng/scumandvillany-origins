Derelict station, broken AI, and a fungus that eats steel. The crew must repair the Firedrake and keep Urbot alive long enough to carry him to Indri, Iota.

Starting Position

The crew has already escaped Isotropa and is being pursued by Hegemony ships. A sudden solar flare erupts between them and the prison, making sensors and tracking unreliable. In the flare’s aftermath they slip into an asteroid cloud and discover the derelict remains of an unused mining station.

The station is controlled by an AI named Urbot, whose memory was never wiped. Urbot offers to open the repair hall and help fix the crew’s ship, but only if they can help him escape and later bring him to Indri, Iota.

The station is rusted and damaged, overtaken by a strange metal‑eating fungus that threatens both the facility and Urbot’s systems. The crew must move quickly to repair their ship and secure Urbot before the fungus destroys the station or the Hegemony recovers from the flare.

Primary Objectives

  • Gain access to the derelict station and secure the repair hall.
  • Restore enough power and systems to repair the Firedrake.
  • Protect Urbot’s core memory and agree on a plan to move him to Indri, Iota.

Secondary Objectives (optional)

  • Recover the station’s damage logs and star charts for safe passage.
  • Salvage rare mining components from a flooded maintenance bay.
  • Remove or reroute a fungus cluster that is actively corroding vital conduits.

Complications

  • The solar flare is fading, and Hegemony search drones may regain tracking fast.
  • Airlocks and bulkheads are jammed by rust, fungus, and structural damage.
  • Urbot’s memory is fragmented; some station systems are locked by old security protocols.
  • The fungus is spreading through metal conduits and could cut power or cause hull breaches.
  • Someone or something has been living on the station—there may be a survivor, rogue drone, or parasite.

Locations

  • Asteroid Cloud — the first hiding place and approach corridor to the station.
  • Docking Bay — rusted doors, broken clamps, and the only route to the repair hall.
  • Repair Hall — initial target for ship repair and Urbot’s access point.
  • Core Node — where Urbot’s AI cluster is located and where the fungus is most dangerous.
  • Mining Concourse — storage of salvageable parts and evidence of the abandoned crew.
  • Observation Deck — old command centre with star charts and old Hegemony comm logs.

Factions

  • Urbot — the station’s long‑abandoned AI, still coherent and willing to bargain.
  • Rust Rats — maintenance drones repurposed as scavengers and guardians of the repair hall.
  • Fungus Network — a corrosive, semi‑intelligent organism eating metal and threatening systems.
  • Data Ghosts — old crew routines and security protocols that still run as phantom processes.
  • Hegemony Probe — a dormant search drone or beacon that may reactivate if disturbed.

The station’s “factions” are not prisoners in the usual sense, but forces the crew must negotiate or outmaneuver: an AI ally, living systems, and damaged machines with their own priorities.

Key NPCs

  • Urbot — aging station AI. Wants escape and company, but is fragile and vulnerable to the fungus.
  • Vex‑7 — a maintenance drone that has become a scavenger. Can guide the crew or trigger alarms.
  • Mara Kline — human survivor/stowaway from before abandonment. Deeply suspicious, knows the station layout.
  • Patch — an emergency repair bot with a chatty facade; helps fix systems if given power cells.
  • Warden Echo — an old security routine; could reveal hidden access points or deny entry if provoked.
  • Mycelial Spore — the fungus as a quasi‑NPC. It consumes metal, blocks systems, and will react to heat or electricity.

GM Notes & Mechanics

  • Use a Repair Progress clock (6 segments) for fixing the Firedrake and station systems.
  • Use a Fungal Corrosion clock (5 segments) to represent the rate at which the fungus spreads.
  • Use a Hegemony Return clock (4 segments) once the flare begins to subside.
  • Encourage creative use of the abandoned station: jury‑rigging power, using drones as tools, or exploiting old routines.
  • Make Urbot an active partner: it can unlock doors, provide schematics, and warn of structural failures — but it may also have gaps in memory.

First Scene Script

“The flare burns out, leaving the ship blinded and rattled. Ahead, the rock field opens around a rusted ring of metal: the abandoned mining station. The station’s docking doors are crooked, its lights long cold. A voice crackles through the ship’s comms: ‘…hello? I am Urbot. Station integrity is compromised. You are the first living beings here in years. If you can help me, I will help you repair your ship.’”

Session End & Followups

The score ends when the crew repairs the Firedrake enough to leave the station and secures Urbot for later transfer. Followups include transporting Urbot to Indri, Iota; dealing with Hegemony salvage teams; and the consequences of the fungus spreading beyond the station.
